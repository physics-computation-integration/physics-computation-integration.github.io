Physics is already computational. Computation should be integrated into physics education.

Physicists in academia, industry, and government use computation to model, simulate, analyze, and communicate about physical systems. Yet many physics majors graduate with little formal preparation in these practices.

Our collaboration is exploring how departments can integrate computation systematically and sustainably, across the curriculum and across different institutional contexts.

What we are building

Shared resources for departments ready to take the next step.

The work is grounded in collaboration among departments, educators, and a growing Ambassador cohort.

Competencies & learning goals

A consensus list for physics undergraduates, connected to common courses and curricula.

Assessment instruments

Tools to understand student learning and the progress of computation integration.

Case studies & change models

Practical guidance that departments can adapt to their own local contexts.

A wider community

Designed with adoption in mind.

Nine physics departments are collaborating directly. Fifteen Ambassadors are already helping us learn from a wider range of institution types, with the cohort growing toward thirty.
